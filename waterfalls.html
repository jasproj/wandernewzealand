Types of Waterfalls

🥾 Hike-In Falls

Trail-access waterfalls reached via short walks (15–90 min). Pack water and weather layers.

🚗 Roadside / Drive-In

Falls visible from the road or with a 5-min walk. Family-friendly, no fitness required.

🚁 Aerial / Cruise Views

Falls only viewable from helicopter, boat, or cruise. Premium price, unique angle.

Best Waterfalls Spots

Sutherland Falls

Fiordland (Milford Track)

One of the tallest in NZ at 580m, three tiers. Reached via the Milford Track or by helicopter day trip.

Huka Falls

Taupo

11m drop with 220,000 litres per second through a narrow chasm. Walkable from Taupo town centre. Jet-boat tours run to the base.

Wairere Falls

Waikato

153m two-tier falls reached via a 90-minute hike through native bush. Lookout at the lower section.

McLean Falls

Catlins (South Island)

22m of cascading falls in beech forest. Easy 40-minute return walk.

Bridal Veil Falls

Waikato (near Raglan)

55m single drop accessible via a 10-minute walk from the carpark. Multiple viewing platforms.

Stirling Falls

Milford Sound

155m falls visible from cruise boats inside Milford. Cruises often nudge the bow under the spray.

Waterfalls Tips

👟 Trail shoes

Most falls hikes have wet, rocky trails. Skip flip-flops.

💧 Water and snacks

Even short hikes burn through water in summer.

🦟 Bug spray

Falls = humid bush = mosquitos. Cover ankles and wrists.

📷 Polarizer

If you're shooting photos, a polarizing filter cuts glare on the falls.
