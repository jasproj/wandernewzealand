Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions from travelers booking through WanderNewZealand.

Do I need a visa to visit New Zealand?

U.S., Canadian, UK, and most EU citizens need an NZeTA (Electronic Travel Authority) before arrival — apply online, costs ~NZ$23, valid 2 years. Plus the International Visitor Conservation Tourism Levy (~NZ$35). Allow 72 hours for processing; rush options exist.

What's the best time of year to visit?

November-April is the NZ summer/autumn — long daylight (sunset 8-9 PM), warm. December-February is peak. May-October is the southern winter; great for skiing in Queenstown and Wanaka, otherwise quiet. Shoulder months (October-November, March-April) are the value sweet spots.

How long do I need on the South Island?

7-10 days minimum if you're combining Queenstown + Milford Sound + Mt Cook + west coast glaciers. South Island distances are deceiving — Queenstown to Milford Sound is 4 hours each way by car or bus.

Are tours weather-dependent?

Some — bungy, paragliding, scenic flights, and helicopter tours frequently reschedule for high winds or low cloud. Boat tours (Milford Sound, Doubtful Sound) run rain or shine — and rain makes the waterfalls more dramatic. Most operators offer free reschedule with 24-hour notice.

What's the cheapest way to do Milford Sound?

A bus + cruise day-trip from Queenstown is the cheapest option (NZ$200-300 per person). Scenic flights are 4-5x more but cut the round-trip from 12 hours to 4. If you have a rental car, drive yourself + book just the cruise (~NZ$100); the road in is one of the most scenic drives in NZ.

Do I tip in New Zealand?

Tipping is uncommon and not expected. Service is usually included in the price. Rounding up or leaving 10% for exceptional service is appreciated but not standard.

How do I get around without a rental car?

InterCity coach network covers most tourist routes; KiwiRail runs scenic trains (Tranz-Alpine, Northern Explorer). For multi-day flexibility, the Stray Bus or Kiwi Experience hop-on-hop-off systems are popular with backpackers. Internal flights (Air NZ, Jetstar) are cheap and fast for long hops.

What's the cancellation policy?

Most tours allow free cancellation 24-48 hours before. Multi-day adventure tours (Routeburn, Milford track guided walks) often require 7-14 days. Weather rescheduling is always free same-day.

Can I do the Routeburn or Milford Track without a guide?

Yes — both are independent walks bookable through DOC (Department of Conservation). Hut bookings open 6+ months ahead and sell out within hours for the popular weekends. Guided walks (Ultimate Hikes, etc.) are pricier but include all gear, food, and lodging.

Will my U.S. cell plan work?

Most U.S. plans charge international roaming (T-Mobile is the exception; Magenta plans include free NZ data at slow speeds). For better speeds, buy a Spark or 2degrees prepaid SIM at the airport (~NZ$30 for 30 days, lots of data).
