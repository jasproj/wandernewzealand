About Milford Sound

Milford Sound (Piopiotahi in Maori) is the most-photographed natural attraction in New Zealand. It's a fjord on the west coast of the South Island, surrounded by 1,200-meter cliffs and accessed by either a 4-hour bus from Queenstown, a 2-hour scenic flight, or a multi-day Routeburn/Milford track hike. Most travelers do the bus-or-fly day trip, which includes a 2-hour cruise on the fjord.

The two most-recommended cruise operators are Real NZ (formerly Real Journeys) and Mitre Peak Cruises. Real NZ has the larger boats and the smoothest ride; Mitre Peak runs smaller boats with a slightly closer view of the cliffs and waterfalls. Both stop at the underwater observatory in Harrison Cove (8 m below the surface, looking out at black coral that grows here at sea level because of the freshwater layer on top). Weather is unpredictable: 200+ rainy days a year is the norm. Cruises run in heavy rain and the waterfalls are at their most dramatic then.

What to know before you book

  • 200+ rain days per year — but cruises run rain or shine, and waterfalls are best when raining
  • Reach via 4-hour bus from Queenstown, 2-hour scenic flight, or multi-day track hike
  • Real NZ and Mitre Peak Cruises are the two main cruise operators
  • Underwater observatory in Harrison Cove — 8m below surface, free with most cruise tickets
  • Best months: November-April (longer daylight); winter cruises are uncrowded but cold
  • Pack layers + waterproof shell regardless of forecast
