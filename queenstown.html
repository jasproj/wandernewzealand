Queenstown Tours

South Island adventure capital — Lake Wakatipu, Milford Sound day-trips, and the highest concentration of bungy/jetboat operators in NZ.

About Queenstown

Queenstown sits on the shores of Lake Wakatipu in the Southern Alps. It's the South Island's adrenaline hub: the original AJ Hackett Bungy site (Kawarau Bridge), the Shotover Jet jetboat, and a dense cluster of skydive, paragliding, and zip-line operators. It's also the launching point for Milford Sound day-trips — a 4-hour bus drive each way, plus a 2-hour cruise — and for Skippers Canyon and Glenorchy 4WD tours.

If you have one day in Queenstown, the highest-value pattern is the Milford Sound full-day combo (bus or fly). If you have two, add a Skippers Canyon morning + a Lake Wakatipu cruise on the TSS Earnslaw (the 1912 steamship) for an afternoon. Queenstown is small enough to walk in 30 minutes corner-to-corner; most adventure operator pickups are at the i-SITE or the Pier on the lakefront.

What to know before you book

  • South Island adventure capital — densest cluster of bungy/jetboat/skydive operators in NZ
  • Milford Sound day-trip is the must-do, but plan a full 12+ hours by bus or 4-5 by fly-cruise
  • TSS Earnslaw (1912 steamship) lake cruises run multiple times daily
  • Shoulder months (October-November, March-April) are quietest; peak is December-February
  • Walkable downtown — most operator meeting points are within 5 min of the i-SITE
  • Weather-dependent — many adventure tours offer free reschedule for high winds
