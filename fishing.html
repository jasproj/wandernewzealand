Types of Fishing

🎣 Inshore / Light Tackle

Half-day shallow-water fishing. Snapper, jacks, and reef species. Family-friendly.

🐟 Offshore / Deep Sea

Full-day blue-water trolling for marlin, mahi, tuna, wahoo. Bigger boats, longer runs.

🏞️ Freshwater / River

Trout, salmon, pike, or coarse fishing depending on region. Wading or boat-based.

Best Fishing Spots

Bay of Islands

Northland

Marlin, kingfish, snapper. The deep water 30km offshore holds NZ's biggest game-fishing tournaments.

Lake Taupo

North Island

Trout fishing capital of NZ — rainbow and brown trout year-round. Fly-fishing or trolling.

Tongariro River

Central North Island

World-renowned trout river. Wading and spinning, multiple access points. Best March-November.

Marlborough Sounds

South Island (top)

Snapper, kingfish, blue cod. Sheltered fishing in Queen Charlotte Sound.

Stewart Island

South of South Island

Blue cod, paua, crayfish. Land-based or boat-based fishing in pristine remote waters.

Kaikoura

South Island (east coast)

Blue cod, hapuka, groper. Deep canyon fishing 30 minutes offshore.

Fishing Tips

🌅 Dawn departures

Most charters leave before sunrise. Plan your sleep accordingly.

🤢 Sea-sickness meds

Even half-day inshore can rough up — take meds the night before.

💵 Tip the mate

Crew is partly tip-funded. 15-20% of charter cost is standard.

🐟 Catch policy

Confirm catch-and-release vs. take-home before booking. Some species are protected.
